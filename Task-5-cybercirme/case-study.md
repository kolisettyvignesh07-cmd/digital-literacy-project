# Cybercrime Case Study

## Introduction
Cybercrime refers to illegal activities carried out using the internet or digital devices. It has become very common in today’s digital world.

## Case Study: Phishing Attack

### What the crime is:

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted organizations (such as banks or online services) to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords, debit/credit card details, or OTPs.

### How it typically happens (step-by-step):

The attacker sends a fake email or SMS pretending to be from a trusted source (e.g., a bank or popular website).
The message creates urgency, such as “Your account will be blocked” or “Suspicious activity detected.”
It includes a link that redirects the user to a fake website that looks identical to the real one.
The victim enters their login credentials or financial details.
The attacker captures this information and may also request an OTP.
Using these details, the attacker gains unauthorized access or performs fraudulent transactions.

### Who is usually targeted:

Phishing commonly targets students, elderly individuals, and people who are not fully aware of online security practices. However, even experienced users can be targeted if the message appears genuine.

### What the consequences are:

The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts. Victims may also experience emotional stress and loss of trust in digital platforms. In serious cases, sensitive personal or professional data may be misused.

## Conclusion
Cybercrime can affect anyone. Awareness and careful online behavior can help prevent such attacks.
